How Much Water Should a Cat Drink Daily?

Water plays a critical role in a cat's overall health. Proper hydration supports kidney function, digestion, circulation, temperature regulation, and urinary tract health.

General Water Intake Recommendations

Most healthy cats require approximately 50 to 60 milliliters of water per kilogram of body weight each day. For example, a 4 kg cat may need around 200–240 ml of water daily.

Factors That Affect Water Consumption

Water requirements vary depending on age, activity level, environment, diet, and overall health. Cats in warm climates or highly active cats may require additional hydration.

Dry Food and Water Intake

Cats fed primarily dry kibble often drink more water because dry food contains very little moisture. Cats eating wet food receive a significant amount of water from their meals.

Signs of Dehydration

  • Dry gums
  • Low energy levels
  • Loss of appetite
  • Sunken eyes
  • Reduced skin elasticity

If severe dehydration is suspected, contact a veterinarian immediately.

Ways to Encourage Drinking

  • Provide fresh water daily.
  • Use multiple water bowls around the home.
  • Consider a cat water fountain.
  • Offer wet food when appropriate.
  • Keep water bowls clean.

Why Hydration Is Important

Adequate hydration helps support kidney function and urinary tract health, both of which become increasingly important as cats age.

Tip: Many cats prefer moving water. A pet fountain may encourage greater daily water intake.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should a cat drink each day?

Most healthy cats require approximately 50–60 ml of water per kilogram of body weight daily.

Do cats eating wet food need less drinking water?

Yes. Wet food contains substantial moisture and contributes to a cat's daily hydration needs.

What are signs of dehydration in cats?

Common signs include dry gums, low energy, loss of appetite, and reduced skin elasticity.

Why do some cats drink very little water?

Cats naturally have a low thirst drive, especially when consuming moisture-rich diets.
