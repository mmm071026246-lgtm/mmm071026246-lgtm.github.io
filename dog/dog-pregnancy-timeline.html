Dog Pregnancy Timeline Week by Week

Dog pregnancy usually lasts around 63 days. Understanding each stage of canine pregnancy can help owners prepare for healthy puppies and recognize important developmental milestones.

Week 1

Fertilization occurs and embryos begin early development. Physical changes are generally not noticeable.

Week 2

Fertilized eggs continue dividing and move toward the uterus for implantation.

Week 3

Embryos attach to the uterine wall and begin receiving nutrients from the mother.

Week 4

Pregnancy can often be confirmed through ultrasound by a veterinarian.

Week 5

Rapid puppy development begins and abdominal enlargement may become noticeable.

Week 6

Weight gain increases and nutritional requirements become more important.

Week 7

Puppies continue growing rapidly. Nesting behavior may begin.

Week 8

Milk production may start and activity levels often decrease.

Week 9

Most dogs deliver puppies around day 63. A comfortable whelping area should be prepared.

Signs Labor Is Near

  • Restlessness
  • Nesting behavior
  • Loss of appetite
  • Drop in body temperature
  • Frequent pacing
  • Seeking isolation
Important: If labor begins prematurely or your dog shows signs of distress, contact a veterinarian immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a dog pregnancy?

Most dog pregnancies last approximately 63 days.

When can pregnancy be confirmed?

Pregnancy can often be confirmed around week 4 with ultrasound.

When does nesting behavior start?

Many dogs begin nesting between weeks 7 and 8.

When should I prepare a whelping box?

Most owners prepare a whelping area during week 7 or week 8.
